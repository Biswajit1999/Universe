# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We — contributors and maintainers — pledge to make participation in UNIVERSE a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the science.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments.
- Publishing others' private information without permission.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scientific integrity

Because UNIVERSE is a science tool, we hold one extra standard: **do not misrepresent data or
results.** Respect the project's core principle of honest provenance labelling
(`Live` / `Demo` / `Estimated` / `Simulated`). Fabricating "live" data or passing model output off
as measured fact is a violation of this Code.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainer.
All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response deemed necessary and
appropriate to the circumstances.
